### Overview

Individual, independent mini-project in Computational Medicine. Students are required to produce a written report in the style of a short conference paper. The project is open-ended and research-led, and students are expected to demonstrate scientific judgement, critical reasoning, and coherence across all stages of the work.

### Task Description

A 4-page conference paper with a maximum of 3 figures, in the style of the template provided (strict requirement — no changes in font or margins).

The topic should be on a clearly defined and medically relevant research question, which includes explicit consideration of sex differences, and which can be investigated using computational methodologies covered in lectures, practicals and classes. This can include (but is not limited to) multiscale modelling and simulation, machine learning for anatomical modelling, parameter inference, and ECG analysis and classification.

### Assessment Criteria

- Clarity of motivation and writing, contextualisation within existing literature
- Consideration of sex differences in the research question
- Originality and novelty of research questions and methodologies
- Description and reproducibility of methodologies
- Coherence and depth of results, including verification, validation and sensitivity analysis
- Relation to concepts discussed throughout the course
- Discussion of results with critical self-evaluation
- Demonstrated understanding, judgement and critical analysis
- Definition of clinical or industrial pathway for impact
